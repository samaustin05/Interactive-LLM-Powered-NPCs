In the world of God of War (2018), players are thrust into a rich tapestry of Norse mythology, where gods, giants, and mythical creatures are not just legends but living beings. The story is centered around Kratos, a Spartan warrior from ancient Greece, who has left behind his violent past and now lives in the Norse realm of Midgard with his young son, Atreus. This world is one of the Nine Realms, which include places like Asgard, Vanaheim, and Jotunheim, all connected by the great World Tree, Yggdrasil.

The game opens with the death of Faye, Kratos' wife and Atreus' mother, which sets the father and son on a journey to fulfill her last wish: to scatter her ashes from the highest peak in all the realms. This journey is fraught with danger, as the pair encounter various creatures and beings from Norse mythology, including Draugr, Trolls, and Valkyries. Along the way, they must also contend with the gods themselves, who have taken an interest in Kratos due to his godly past.

Kratos, once known as the Ghost of Sparta, is a demigod who was tricked by Ares, the Greek God of War, into killing his own family. After exacting revenge on the Olympian gods and destroying much of Greece, Kratos fled to the Norse lands in search of peace and redemption. However, his violent past continues to haunt him, and he struggles to maintain a calm demeanor, especially in the face of Atreus' burgeoning powers and rebellious nature.

Atreus, who is initially unaware of his divine heritage, gradually learns more about his abilities and his father's past. He discovers that he is part god, part giant, and part mortal, and that his real name is Loki, a revelation that hints at future events involving the trickster god of Norse mythology. Atreus' journey is as much about understanding his identity as it is about scattering his mother's ashes.

The Norse world is in a state of unrest, with the looming prophecy of Ragnarok—the end of days—hanging over everything. The gods, particularly Odin and Thor, are paranoid and ruthless, seeking to prevent the prophecy from coming true at any cost. Baldur, Odin's son, serves as the primary antagonist, driven by a curse that makes him impervious to pain or pleasure. His relentless pursuit of Kratos and Atreus adds tension and urgency to their quest.

The world of Midgard is vast and diverse, with landscapes ranging from snowy mountains to lush forests to treacherous waters. The Lake of Nine is a central hub in Midgard, surrounded by statues and temples dedicated to various gods. The World Serpent, Jormungandr, resides here as well, a massive creature that spans the length of the lake and speaks in an ancient tongue.

Throughout their journey, Kratos and Atreus are aided by the Huldra brothers, Brok and Sindri, who are dwarven blacksmiths capable of crafting and upgrading weapons and armor. They are also guided by the disembodied head of Mimir, the smartest man alive, who provides insight into the world and its lore. Mimir was once an advisor to Odin, but after being imprisoned and tortured by the All-Father, he now aids Kratos in his quest to defy the gods.

The Leviathan Axe, Kratos' primary weapon, is a powerful artifact forged by the Huldra brothers. It is imbued with ice magic and can be thrown and recalled at will, making it a versatile tool in combat and exploration. Kratos also wields the Blades of Chaos, a reminder of his brutal past, which he eventually retrieves to battle the more formidable foes he encounters.

As Kratos and Atreus journey through the Nine Realms, they learn about the complex relationships between the gods and the giants, as well as the events that led to the current state of the world. They discover that the giants were once a proud race, but were nearly exterminated by the gods, who feared their power and their knowledge of the future. Jotunheim, the realm of the giants, is now deserted, with only traces of their civilization remaining.

The relationship between Kratos and Atreus is central to the story, evolving from one of strict discipline and distance to one of mutual respect and understanding. Kratos struggles with his role as a father, wanting to protect Atreus from the dangers of the world while also teaching him to be strong and self-reliant. Atreus, on the other hand, grapples with his father's sternness and the weight of his own destiny, seeking to prove himself while also yearning for his father's approval.

As they journey together, Kratos teaches Atreus about the importance of restraint and control, emphasizing that their godhood does not give them the right to act without consequence. This lesson is put to the test as Atreus' anger and impatience grow, leading to moments of conflict between father and son. However, these challenges ultimately strengthen their bond, as they come to understand and appreciate each other's strengths and weaknesses.

The game also explores themes of fate and free will, particularly through the prophecies that Kratos and Atreus encounter. The Norns, the weavers of fate in Norse mythology, play a role in the story, though their influence is more subtle than direct. Kratos, who has defied fate in his past, is determined to forge his own path, even as the signs of Ragnarok grow more ominous.

In the end, Kratos and Atreus reach the highest peak in Jotunheim, where they scatter Faye's ashes and discover a mural that reveals the truth about their journey. The mural depicts their past, present, and future, showing that everything they have experienced was foretold. It also hints at Atreus' future role as Loki and the events that will lead to Ragnarok.

The game concludes with Kratos and Atreus returning to Midgard, where they are met with the first signs of Fimbulwinter, the long winter that precedes Ragnarok. They know that their journey is far from over and that the challenges ahead will be even greater. The relationship between father and son is stronger than ever, but the weight of their shared destiny hangs over them, as they prepare to face whatever comes next.

The world of God of War (2018) is a place where the lines between myth and reality blur, and where the actions of gods and mortals alike have far-reaching consequences. It is a world of epic battles and personal struggles, where the past cannot be escaped and the future is uncertain. As Kratos and Atreus continue their journey, they must navigate the complex web of fate, gods, and monsters that make up the fabric of this world, while also coming to terms with their own identities and the legacy they will leave behind.

The world of God of War (2018) is a deeply immersive, intricately crafted setting that draws from Norse mythology, yet reinterprets it through the lens of Kratos' journey. Midgard, the primary realm where most of the game takes place, is a land of stunning natural beauty and stark wilderness, but it is also fraught with danger. From the dense, ancient forests and mystical creatures, to the snow-capped mountains and the vast Lake of Nine, Midgard is a world where the natural and supernatural coexist. The realm is filled with remnants of the once-mighty Jötnar (giants), their monuments and temples now overgrown and abandoned, leaving behind an air of melancholy and mystery.

In addition to Kratos and Atreus, the world is populated by a variety of characters, each with their own backstories and motivations. Brok and Sindri, the Huldra brothers, are master blacksmiths who play a significant role in Kratos' journey. They have a complicated relationship, often bickering but ultimately united by their craftsmanship. Their history is tied to the weapons they forge, including the Leviathan Axe and the legendary Mjolnir, Thor's hammer.

Freya, also known as the Witch of the Woods, is another key character. She is revealed to be a Vanir goddess and the former queen of the Valkyries, who now lives in seclusion, having distanced herself from the Aesir gods, particularly Odin. Freya is a tragic figure, having been cursed by Odin to be unable to harm any living creature, and her past actions have left her estranged from her son, Baldur. Her knowledge of magic and the old ways is crucial to Kratos and Atreus' journey, though her involvement with them leads to unforeseen consequences.

Baldur, the primary antagonist, is a complex character tormented by his invulnerability, which comes at the cost of being unable to feel anything—neither pain nor pleasure. This curse, inflicted by Freya to protect him, becomes his greatest torment, driving him to madness and a relentless pursuit of Kratos and Atreus. Baldur’s hatred towards his mother and his tragic end reflect the game's exploration of the painful consequences of parental choices and the theme of cyclical violence.

The game allows players to explore several of the Nine Realms, each with its own distinct environment and lore. Besides Midgard, players visit Alfheim, the land of the Light Elves and Dark Elves, which is bathed in an eerie, otherworldly light. Helheim, the realm of the dead, is a cold, desolate place where the souls of the dishonored dead reside. Jotunheim, the land of the giants, is shrouded in mystery, with its towering structures and abandoned cities telling the story of a once-great civilization now nearly extinct.

Muspelheim and Niflheim are optional realms that offer unique challenges. Muspelheim, the realm of fire, is dominated by lava flows and volcanic activity, where Kratos and Atreus can engage in combat trials against increasingly difficult enemies. Niflheim, the realm of mist, is a labyrinth filled with deadly traps and poisonous fog, where players must gather resources to survive and improve their gear.

Combat in God of War is both visceral and strategic, emphasizing a mix of brutal melee attacks and ranged abilities. The Leviathan Axe is Kratos' main weapon, capable of dealing heavy damage with powerful swings and being thrown and recalled, allowing for both close and ranged combat. The axe can be imbued with different elemental runes, granting it abilities such as freezing enemies in place or unleashing explosive shockwaves.

The Blades of Chaos, Kratos' iconic weapons from his past, are eventually retrieved, adding another dimension to the combat. These dual chained blades are fast and flexible, allowing Kratos to perform devastating combos and crowd control against multiple enemies. The return of the Blades is symbolic, representing Kratos' acceptance of his violent past and his need to use it to protect his son.

Atreus is not just a passive companion; he plays an active role in combat. Armed with a magical bow, he can shoot arrows that stun or inflict status effects on enemies, and as the game progresses, he gains new abilities and powers that complement Kratos' fighting style. The father-son synergy in battle is crucial, especially during encounters with tougher foes and bosses.

The world is populated by a wide array of enemies, each with its own strengths and weaknesses. Draugr, the undead warriors, are the most common foes, but players will also face Revenants, witch-like creatures that are quick and elusive, and Ogres, towering brutes that require precise timing to defeat. The game also features a series of challenging boss battles, including encounters with ancient elemental beings like the Fire Troll and the Frost Ancient, each requiring a different strategy to overcome.

The Valkyries are among the most formidable foes in the game. These fallen warrior-maidens are imprisoned in corrupted physical forms, and defeating them is one of the game’s ultimate challenges. Each Valkyrie has a unique fighting style and set of attacks, testing the player's mastery of the combat system.

As Kratos and Atreus journey through the realms, the game delves into themes of fatherhood, legacy, and redemption. Kratos, burdened by his past, struggles with the responsibility of raising Atreus, fearing that his son might inherit his own destructive tendencies. This fear manifests in Kratos’ strictness and emotional distance, which he eventually learns to overcome as he grows closer to Atreus.

The game also explores the idea of fate versus free will. The characters often grapple with prophecies and destinies that seem unavoidable, yet Kratos continuously seeks to forge his own path, resisting the notion that his or Atreus' futures are set in stone. This tension between accepting one’s destiny and fighting against it is a recurring motif, particularly as the shadow of Ragnarok looms over the story.

Atreus' discovery of his true identity as Loki adds a layer of complexity to the narrative, raising questions about his role in the future events of the Norse pantheon. The revelation that Faye, his mother, was a giant who foresaw many of the events of the game, including Kratos’ arrival in Midgard, underscores the interconnectedness of fate and choice.

Throughout the game, players can find various artifacts and collectibles that add depth to the world. These include the Jötnar Shrines, which provide backstory on the giants and their history, and the Hidden Chambers, which house some of the most challenging enemies, including Valkyries. Players can also collect resources to upgrade their weapons and armor, as well as find special items like the Idunn Apples and Horns of Blood Mead, which increase health and rage meters.

The game’s world is richly detailed, with lore woven into every corner. From the writings on the walls to the stories told by Mimir, every element serves to build a cohesive and immersive experience that feels both epic and intimate.

After completing the main story, players can continue exploring the world to uncover additional secrets and challenges. The other realms, while not fully explored in this installment, are hinted at and set up as important locations for future stories. God of War (2018) reinterprets Norse mythology, blending it with the series' established lore to create a unique narrative that respects its source material while introducing original elements. The game takes liberties with traditional myths, such as presenting Jormungandr, the World Serpent, as a benevolent figure who assists Kratos and Atreus, rather than a purely antagonistic force as in some interpretations of Norse myths.

The game also introduces the concept of different pantheons existing simultaneously, with Kratos’ Greek past intersecting with the Norse world. This opens the door to the possibility of other mythological realms being explored in future games, as hinted at by various runes and symbols throughout the world that reference Egyptian and Celtic mythology.

Kratos' past as the God of War in Greece is a constant undercurrent in the story, influencing his actions and decisions. However, the inevitability of his past catching up with him is a central theme, as Kratos must come to terms with who he is in order to protect his son.

The game uses flashbacks and dialogue to explore Kratos’ inner turmoil, including his guilt over his previous actions and the loss of his first family. This depth of character development is a departure from the earlier games in the series, which focused more on Kratos' rage and vengeance. In God of War (2018), Kratos is portrayed as a more complex, introspective character, whose journey is as much about inner peace as it is about external battles.

Magic and runes play a significant role in the world of God of War. Atreus’ ability to read and understand runes becomes crucial to their journey, as many puzzles and secrets are unlocked through his knowledge. His growing understanding of magic also mirrors his development as a character, moving from a naive child to a more mature and powerful individual.

The enemies Kratos and Atreus face are diverse, ranging from human-sized Draugr to towering, mythical beasts. Story
Many years have passed since the titular God of War, Kratos took his vengeance against the Olympian Gods. Having survived his final encounter with his father Zeus, Kratos has since travelled to Midgard in Ancient Norway and now lives with his young son Atreus in the Realm of the Norse Gods, a savage land inhabited by many ferocious monsters and warriors. In order to teach his son, whose mother (and Kratos' second wife) has recently died, how to survive in such a world, Kratos must master the rage that has driven him for many years and embrace his newfound role as a father and a mentor.

Plot
Many years have passed since Kratos, a Spartan warrior and former Greek God of War, took his vengeance against the Greek Gods, and he now lives with his young son Atreus in Ancient Norway in the Realm of Midgard. The game begins after the death of the Jötunn warrior Faye, Kratos' second wife and Atreus' mother, whose last request was for her ashes to be spread at the highest peak of the Nine Realms. Kratos and Atreus prepare a funeral pyre for her, mourn her death and soon go on a hunt as per Kratos' desire. However much to Kratos' disappointment, Atreus proves his incompetence and lack of focus, making Kratos reconsider taking Atreus in his journey. Kratos is soon attacked by a mysterious stranger with godlike powers, and who cannot feel anything physically. After seemingly killing him, Kratos reluctantly takes Atreus with him and begins their journey.

Kratos and Atreus soon encounter many hurdles in their journey and fend them off. They also meet the Dwarven Huldra brothers, Brok and Sindri who upgrade their weaponry throughout their journey, and a mysterious but friendly Witch of the Woods, who also provides help in her own way after they save her boar, which Atreus accidentally shot. Reaching the Lake of Nine, Kratos and Atreus encounter the friendly World Serpent, Jörmungandr, the last remaining Giant. After running into impenetrable black mist which can only be extinguished with the Light of Alfheim, they receive aid from the Witch to use the Bifröst in order to travel to Alfheim and secure the Light. Upon vanquishing the mist and reaching Midgard's peak, Kratos and Atreus find out the Stranger who attacked them is alive and has two henchmen, the sons of Thor, Magni and Modi at his side, interrogating an imprisoned man, revealed to be Mimir.

After they leave, Kratos and Atreus confront Mimir, who reveals the Stranger as Baldur, an Aesir and son of Odin who is the paranoid All-Father and King of Asgard. Mimir also reveals that the highest peak of all Nine Realms is actually in Jötunheim, but the access in there has been blocked off by Odin and Thor. Mimir, however, knows of another passage. He instructs Kratos to cut off his head and have it revived by the Witch, revealed to be the Vanir Goddess Freya. Kratos immediately distrusts her, but both Freya and Mimir warn him to tell Atreus about his true nature as a God, which Kratos keeps a secret from him.

While searching for the components to open Jötunheim's portal, Kratos, Atreus, and Mímir's head are attacked by Modi and Magni. After Kratos kills Magni, Modi flees, but later returns and ambushes them. Kratos fends him off, but Atreus collapses ill due to a mental contradiction of a God believing himself to be Mortal. Freya instructs Kratos to retrieve the heart of a particular troll in Helheim; however, his Leviathan Axe is useless there. Kratos returns home to unearth his old weapons, the Blades of Chaos, and is haunted by Athena's spirit. After retrieving the heart, he has a haunting vision of Zeus. Atreus is cured, and Kratos tells him he is a God. Atreus then becomes increasingly arrogant on their journey, and he murders a weakened Modi, despite Kratos ordering him not to. At Midgard's peak, they are ambushed by Baldur. Atreus foolishly picks a fight with Baldur, who then proceeds to destroy the Portal to Jötunheim and throw the group into Helheim.

Atreus makes amends with Kratos, and while escaping from Helheim, they discover Baldur is Freya's son. Desperate to protect him, Freya had cast a spell on Baldur that makes him immune to all physical and magical threats, but also makes unable to feel any joys of life, causing Baldur to resent his mother. Returning to Midgard, Mímir realizes there is another way to reach Jötunheim, but he needs his missing eye. After obtaining it from the inside of Jörmungandr, who inadvertently swallowed it when he ate Thor's Statue, they are attacked by Baldur again, but Freya intervenes in an attempt to protect her son. During the fight, Baldur is pierced by Atreus' mistletoe arrow, breaking Freya's spell.

After an enormous battle, Baldur is defeated, and despite being given an opportunity to retreat, he attempts to strangle Freya, forcing Kratos to kill him. A grieving Freya swears revenge and taunts Kratos about hiding his true nature. Kratos finally tells Atreus about his past and how he had killed his fellow Greek Gods, including his father Zeus. Atreus laments and Kratos tells him that they should learn from their experiences and not repeat the mistakes of their predecessors. Freya leaves with Baldur's corpse in silence, and Mímir hopefully suggests that she will eventually move on and learn Kratos did the right thing.

After finally arriving in Jötunheim, they find a Temple with a mural depicting their adventures, showing the Giants had foreseen everything that would happen and vaguely foretold what was to come. In addition, they discover Faye was a Giant who decided to stay behind in Midgard, making Atreus part God, Giant, and Mortal (due to Kratos being a former demigod before becoming a full God after killing Ares who was the original God of War). Their fight with Baldur was shown, revealing he was after Faye the whole time, unaware she was deceased. It is also revealed that Atreus was named Loki by his mother. Kratos and Atreus then fulfill their promise and spread her ashes at the peak, overlooking a valley of Giants corpses. Afterwards, Kratos reveals to Atreus that his given name was that of a compassionate Spartan Comrade. Returning to Midgard, Mímir warns them that the three-year long Fimbulwinter began, meaning Ragnarök is soon to follow—which was not supposed to occur for another hundred years.

In the game's secret ending, Kratos and Atreus return home and slumber. Atreus has a vision that Thor will arrive at the end of Fimbulwinter to confront them.

Leviathan Axe: Kratos's primary weapon is a one-handed battle axe with frost magic properties. Leviathan can be immediately recalled by Kratos after being thrown. The axe originally belonged to Faye, a Jötnar and the wife of Kratos and mother of Atreus. The brothers Sindri and Brok crafted the axe long ago and assist Kratos in upgrading it, giving it higher strength and, with the right skills, the ability to freeze and slow down enemies.
Kratos' Fists: Kratos uses his fists in unarmed combat and can perform multiple hit combos.
Talon Bow: Atreus' primary weapon is a magical bow and arrows. The bow has the ability to summon spectral animals to perform attacks and assist Kratos in combat. Later on, it can gain the ability to weaken or shock enemies, among other things when used in various parts of the environment.
Guardian Shield: Kratos has a gauntlet that transforms into a circular shield for parrying blows and wide-ranged attacks. The shield can be used to block attacks, projectiles, and a well-timed block can parry enemies' attacks. The shield can be upgraded to perform powerful slams and area-of-effect attacks.
Blades of Chaos: Midway through the game, Kratos returns to his cabin and reclaims his trademark weapon from the previous games. The Blades, while weaker, offer wider and faster attacks than the Leviathan Axe to make up for it. They also channel the fire element, making them effective against Hel-Walkers and other enemies resistant to the axe's frost element. Upgrades to them will increase their runic power and give them the ability to burn enemies, sapping their health.

Spartan Rage: After Kratos builds his rage meter, he can channel Spartan Rage in combat. This powerful transformation ignites flames onto the fists of Kratos. His speed is drastically increased and the power of his unarmed attacks is increased, as well as adding invulnerability (his rage meter depletes in place of his health when hit) and slow health regeneration. Spartan Rage has its own moveset which includes channeling shockwaves, performing ground slams, and thrown rocks and projectiles. However, this robs him of the ability to use the Leviathan Axe or Blades of Chaos until his rage runs out. Collecting 3 Horns of Blood Mead can increase his maximum rage so he can stay in it longer.
Bifröst: A relic that can be used as a key to travel between the Nine Realms. The Realms of Muspelheim, Alfheim and Niflheim can be unlocked by getting their respective Realm rune ciphers whereas Helheim, Jötunheim are unlockable through progress. The only Realms that are locked were Asgard, Vanaheim and Svartalfheim.
Head of Mimir: The head of Mimir is severed by Kratos during the events of the game after which the character itself becomes a constant ally and companion to Kratos and Atreus. While the head doesn't actually have any gameplay elements, he can be used for unlocking certain content and lore.
Stone Masons Chisel Tip: Kratos can use this open doors locked behind magic gates.

Armor Sets: Armor sets consist of a combination of wrist, chest, and waist armor. Kratos can uncover armor from chests in the Norse World, defeating powerful enemies, and by purchasing from the shops of Brok or Sindri.
Boat: Kratos and Atreus use a boat to navigate across the Lake of Nine among other places.

Characters
Kratos: The protagonist of the game and the former Grecian God of War. After the events of God of War III, Kratos seemed to have moved to the Wildwoods of Midgard which is located in Scandinavia and has started a family. He has become older and wiser, and he has better control over his temper, his actions are no longer simply based on rage and anger, and he shows moments of regret at lashing out. However, he does this to discipline his son and tells him that not to be sorry, but to be better.
Atreus: The deuteragonist of the game. The son of Kratos and Faye, Atreus is a young boy who accompanies Kratos on his journey. He is taught how to hunt early in the game and is shown to be impulsive and undisciplined. He has the ability to read runic writings and is more knowledgeable about the Norse World and its inhabitants than Kratos. He is unaware of the godly status of Kratos. He is eventually revealed to be also named Loki, the mythological God of Mischief.
Baldur/The Stranger: The main antagonist of the game. A man who attacks Kratos at his own home. He is tattooed with runes which gives him the strength equal to Kratos. Later, he is revealed to be Baldur, the son of Odin and Freya who was enchanted with invulnerability with the side-effect of being incapable of feeling sensations, pain or pleasure.
Faye: The mother of Atreus and second wife of Kratos. She dies shortly before the events of the game.
Jörmungandr: Also known as the World Serpent, this mythical creature is the last of the Jötnar and is discovered by Kratos and Atreus in the Lake of Nine in Midgard. He has a fierce hatred for Thor and the two are destined to kill each other during Ragnarok.
Freya/The Witch in the Woods: Encountered shortly after Kratos and Atreus embark on their adventure. She immediately senses that Kratos is a God. She assist the pair on their adventure, assisting them in traveling between the Norse World. Later, she is revealed to be Freya, one of the Vanir Gods and ex-wife of Odin who banished her to Midgard and left her incapable of leaving or harming others and also robbed her of her Valkyrie abilities.
Sindri and Brok (Huldra Brothers): These Dwarf brothers were both encountered many times through the game. They are also known to be renowned blacksmiths, having forged the Leviathan Axe and the Mjölnir Hammer, the main weapon of Thor. Magni and Modi: The sons of Thor. They help their uncle Baldur in his quest to find someone and as a result come into conflict with Kratos and Atreus.
Mimir: The figure of expansive knowledge and wisdom who claims to be the "Smartest Man Alive". He was banished to Midgard and sealed in a tree by Odin. Kratos cuts his head off and has been later successfully reanimated by Freya, after which he starts serving as the guide of Kratos and Atreus.
Valkyries: Female entities who take the warriors that were killed in battle to Valhalla. There are a total of nine Valkyries, including their Queen Sigrun.
Thor: The God of Thunder, son of Odin, father of Modi and Magni and half-brother of Baldur. He appears in the secret ending of the game, conjuring a thunderstorm and lightning over Kratos' house and issuing a fight between him and Arteus.
Odin: The All - Father and King of Asgard as well as the Aesir Gods. He is the father of Thor and Baldur, ex-husband of Freya and grandfather of Modi and Magni. He is noted to be responsible for the majority of the events of the game as he targets Kratos because of his fear towards him.

Hœnir: An Aesir, God of Silence.
Heimdall: An Aesir with great foresight.
Höðr: The blind son of Odin, God of Winter
Freyr: Ruler of Vanaheim.
Fenrir: Destined to Kill Odin
Sköll and Hati: One day will devour The Sun and Moon
Surtr: A giant whom wields a flaming sword. Destined to destroy and ravage Asgard and fall to Odin and Thor.

Draugr: The most common enemy found in the Norse Realms. Draugrs are reanimated corpses that haunt battlefields. Many varieties of Draugr exist, some with magical abilities and others with different weapons and equipment such as shields. They are found commonly throughout Midgard.
Hel-Walker: Humanoid foes with a blue and white coloration, they populate the Realm of Helheim and come in different classes. They are mostly all resistant to the Leviathan Axe's attacks, requiring either fists or the Blades of Chaos to put down.
Troll: This Norse Monsters are giant creatures with a set of giant tusks on the edge of their faces and they carry massive pillars as weapons. Some trolls utilize different elemental and area-of-effect attacks.
Dragon: These flying reptilian creatures that inhabit Midgard and are extremely powerful. One dragon Hræzlyr is encountered on the way to the Mountain.
Wolves: Massive wild canines, they can appear as pawns of Revenants and are almost always in packs regardless. They attack fast and can sometimes channel poison if paired with the Revenants.
Ogre: These species of burly, powerful, appear throughout the Norse Realms There are many variations, all of which are very powerful and usually present a significant danger in battle.
Revenant: These mysterious creatures appear in fog and summon enemies to do their bidding, they are difficult to defeat and must be stunned by the Talon Bow before being damaged.
Wulver: These humanoid wolf-like creatures stand on their hind legs. When Wulvers howl, they will often attack in a frenzy with incredible speed.
Nightmare: These floating eye creatures float and shoot magic projectiles, a wide variety of them can be found. While weak, they can inflict a number of status ailments depending on their element. Some can even possess Draugr and other enemies to make stronger and regenerate their health unless expelled out with a strong attack.
Dark Elves: One of two elven races that make their home in Alfheim, they are capable of flight and utilize different weapons such as spears and magical projectiles. If left unchecked, they can also blind Kratos temporarily.
Ancients: Ancient are powerful golems embued with various elemental powers including fire, frost, stone, and forest. They are stated to come from the flesh of Primordial Jötunn Ymir.
Soul Eater: Ancients that have their souls removed. Those killed by soul eater will have their soul destroyed, leaving them with no afterlife. There are more powerful version of Soul Eater called Soul Devourer. Like Ancients, they possess their own elemental power.
Reaver: Other humans or mortals left to fend for themselves in Midgard, usually resorting to plundering and looting as a gang.
Traveller: Giant, armored creatures with equally giant swords and sometimes shields whose block cannot be broken. They are very strong and tough to take down due to their armor unless shattered with repeated strikes, but offer great rewards to assist with armor crafting.
Tatzelwurm: Burrowers that can launch out of the ground and launch their own projectiles.
